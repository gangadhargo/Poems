  1. On the terrace

    As the rain gently subsides, The stars emerge from their hiding places in the plain sky, A celestial light show to brighten up the night. Amidst the Margosa trees, a cool breeze Brings relief to the whole terrace, Driving away mosquitoes and flies with its gentle touch. In the distance, people chant and pray to their gods, searching for something. An infant cries out, but his mother's soothing voice calms him down. Dogs barked in the night, and for a moment, It seemed like they might be trying to protect the village. Gradually, all sounds fade into silence as the whole village falls asleep.

  2. Unknown Girl

    At the airport, I met a beautiful young girl who was braiding her silky black hair so that it would not fall onto her shoulders.

    We talked about our pasts as we looked for our flights and had no idea what the future held.

    Eventually, we left to catch our respective departures without saying goodbye.

    We never meet the present; life is all about exchanging stories from the past, never knowing what is in store,

    while dying without learning what the present holds.

  3. Subtle Violence

    A flock of sheep was crossing the road, causing the impatient humans on their bikes to blare their horns. The sheep were bleating in confusion and fear. Wasn't this violence? Many people did not recognise it as such.

    A young boy was playfully swatting a dog with a stick, leaving the animal running around in confusion as it didn't understand why he was being beaten. Again, wasn't this violence?

    On the shore of a river, there was a temple where people chanted loudly to their god each evening, disturbing the calm waters. Wasn't this noise pollution a form of violence?

    Any activity done to escape oneself can be seen as an act of violence against the present moment - lo, we are easily ignorant.

  4. Slippery of thought

    As one strolls along the road, green meadows stretch far and wide. Taking in the beautiful verdant scene in silence, a thought suddenly pops up that proves insignificant.

    The realisation of its own insignificance causes it to linger for some time. One gets lost in this thought, forgetting all about the present moment;

    however, when understanding its lack of importance, the thought slowly vanishes.

    All of sudden one is aware that one among houses. It's remarkable how easy it is to divert focus away from the here and now onto an inconsequential thought.

  5. Die as

    People wish to pass away

    as the leader, as a creator, as an attorney, as a physician, as an employee, as a scientist, as a dad, as a mom, as a husband, as a wife, as an adored son, as an adored daughter, as wealthier, as less fortunate, as famous, as someone who achieved great things, as someone who imagines great things, as someone who has faith in something bigger than themselves, as someone who follows the lead of others, as an inhabitant of Earth,

    But of course never as Nobody.

  6. A Beautiful Evening

    The winding road was surrounded by lush pastures, and the sun slowly sank lower in the sky as the village descended into darkness.

    A cool breeze rustled through the air, and the unmistakable sound of peacocks calling and frogs hopping off the pavement filled the air.

    Everyone was rushing to get home for the night, while an old couple smiled at each other as they took a stroll.

    Everything that had happened in this moment seemed to have been frozen in time, only to be remembered later when someone chose to write it down on paper.

    But could one ever capture a moment so perfectly with abstraction? The duality of life began here.

  7. Suddenness

    At the beach, a sloshing sound signalled that it was starting to rain. The men embraced the waves and were overjoyed by the rain.

    The waves crashed against the shore before slipping away into their source. Tiny crabs scurried around their tiny holes on the shore while people hid under trees to keep from getting drenched.

    When the rain stopped suddenly, an eerie silence surrounded the trees.

    People started walking home while crabs resumed crawling along the sand.

    What causes this suddenness? Can one's brain ever truly answer it?
